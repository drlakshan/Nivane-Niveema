# Five Hindrances

## Concept
The five hindrances are mental obstacles that obstruct clarity, concentration, and liberating insight. In this corpus, the clearest practical discussion is linked to *dhammānupassanā* in the *Satipaṭṭhāna* framework and to the work of right endeavour.

## The five
1. Sensual desire
2. Ill will
3. Sloth and torpor
4. Restlessness and worry
5. Doubt

## Important notes
- Sermon 31 discusses the hindrances under *dhammānupassanā* and connects them to right effort.
- Sermon 15 links discursive thought with the abandoning of mental hindrances.
- Not every sermon lists all five individually, so concept questions should consult both this topic file and the cited sermons.

## Citation guide
Prefer citing Sermon 31 when the question is about practical observation and abandonment of hindrances.
